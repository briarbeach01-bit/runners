Beginner Tips (no jargon)

If you remember only one thing: start easy. Fitness comes from consistency, not suffering.

1) Run slow enough to talk

If you can’t speak a full sentence, you’re going too hard for an “easy run.”

2) Walk breaks are not failure

Run 1–3 minutes, walk 1 minute, repeat. You still build fitness.

3) Don’t increase too fast

Add a little at a time. Your lungs improve faster than your joints and tendons.

4) Your shoes should feel “okay”

You don’t need the most expensive pair. You need a pair that doesn’t hurt your feet.

5) Rest is training

Rest days prevent injury and help you get faster. They are part of the plan.
